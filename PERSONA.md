## Demographics

- Age: 20–40
- Gender: All genders
- Location: Large and medium-sized cities
- Occupation: Individuals working in or interested in Arts or Information Technology
- Income: Not a primary factor — the application is open-source and focused on educational and artistic purposes
- Tech Level: Intermediate — comfortable with mobile apps and social media, but not technical

## Profile

- A person interested in modern and digital art
- Someone who enjoys traveling and exploring new places
- Curious about digital ownership (e.g., NFTs), but not necessarily experienced
- Someone interested in maps, geography, and spatial experiences
- Curious and exploratory
- Creative but not necessarily an artist
- Enjoys discovering new tools and experiences
- Values simplicity and instant results

## Goals

- Create unique digital artworks
- Capture and remember places they visit
- Contribute to a shared digital exhibition
- Express creativity through location-based experiences
- Creating something unique and personal
- Sharing experiences visually
- Exploring creativity in a simple way

### Frustrations

- Apps that are too complex or slow
- Apps with a lot of ads or that requires paying to use
- Apps offering free services only to stole your data
- Too many steps before seeing results
- Lack of visual feedback
- Not understanding what the final result will look like

## Behaviors

- Uses the app during daily routines to create art from familiar places (e.g., office, university)
- Opens the app when exploring new areas in the city to capture something new
- Uses the app while traveling to create artistic souvenirs of visited locations
- Enjoys creating art in remote or unique locations to produce rare or exclusive pieces
- Sometimes creates art purely for creative expression or participation in exhibitions
- Often used outdoors (walking, traveling)
- Frequently used with one hand
- Sometimes used with limited internet connection
- Short interaction sessions (30 seconds to 2 minutes)

## Key Scenarios

- Quickly opening the app to generate and save an artwork locally
- Browsing a personal gallery of created artworks, with options to share
- Customizing the style and parameters of the generated artwork
- Learning more about the Martp project and its purpose
- Providing permissions and optional personal details to contribute to a public exhibition

### Success Criteria

- Can create an artwork in less than 10 seconds
- Feels surprised or delighted by the result
- Wants to share or save the artwork
- Returns to the app multiple times per week

## Design Implications

- Frictionless onboarding: The user should be able to generate their first artwork within seconds of opening the app
- Playful and light tone: The app should feel fun, creative, and engaging — not like an enterprise tool
- Visual-first experience: Prioritize icons, previews, and animations over text-heavy interfaces
- Encourage creativity and repetition: Motivate users to create more artworks through feedback, rewards, or visual progression
